Facemeet A facial-recognition system

FaceMeet is a facial-recognition based attendance web application initially designed for the need of Icosnet Company with a main purpose of keep track of their employees attendance in meetings

Key Challenges and Solutions

    • User Experience (UX) Design:simple use for application, Conduct user meeting, use design principles, and conduct usability testing.
    • Ensuring the system accurately recognizes employees under different lighting conditions, camera angles, and variations in appearance.
    • Maintaining system performance while handling large datasets, multiple employees, and real-time attendance tracking, especially for large organizations like Icosnet.
    • This was our first experience working with AI, which meant we had to learn and apply new concepts such as model generation, training, and fine-tuning from scratch. The lack of prior expertise in AI and machine learning added to the complexity of the project.

My Roles and Contributions

  • Frontend Development: I designed and developed the entire user interface and frontend components in just 3 days, ensuring the application was both user-friendly and visually appealing.
  • Integration: I established the connection between the frontend and the AI models, ensuring seamless communication for real-time facial recognition functionality.
  • Model Training and Data Collection: I was actively involved in training the models, collecting and preparing the necessary datasets, and optimizing their performance for our use case.
  • Code Refactoring: I improved and optimized the codebase, making it cleaner, more efficient, and easier to maintain.
  • Memory Optimization: I contributed to reducing memory usage in the system, addressing what our director described as a challenge worthy of a doctoral thesis.

Lessons Learned

  • Know your customers and what they need.
  • Use technology that works well and is easy to use.
  • Listen to feedback and make changes to improve the application.

Facemeet was a successful project that helped people in Icosnet control meetings more easily. I'm proud of my contributions to this project and the positive impact it had.
